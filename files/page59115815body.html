Managing Director and Test Manager
He has completed all testing and quality management tasks in accordance with the international ISTQB standard. His career started as a traditional software tester, but his continuous development gradually led him to become an agile tester, test coordinator, test automation engineer and finally test manager and project manager.

Over the past 17 years, he has made his extensive practical experience available to customers. He currently takes on additional responsibility as an ISTQB teacher at the WAMOCON Academy and leads various workshops ranging from software testing to personal mentoring on testing and quality management.

Valery not only supports prospective professionals, but also conducts seminars for IT project teams with the aim of continuously improving the quality of IT systems.

This enables his clients to achieve their goals faster and more efficiently.
He is currently a leading expert in the successful planning and implementation of IT test management projects with a team of up to 450 people.

Daniel Moretz
Test manager, test automation specialist and ISTQB trainer
Daniel Moretz is an experienced test manager, test coordinator and ISTQB trainer with over 7 years of experience in application lifecycle management. He has worked as a test manager for 3 years and as an ISTQB trainer for 3 years, which has given him in-depth knowledge of functional and non-functional testing, load and performance testing and test automation.

His main tasks include the planning, preparation, coordination and reporting of test activities. Daniel is responsible for the creation of test cases, the definition of test data and the execution of integration tests, system tests, acceptance tests and regression tests. In his role as test manager, he ensures smooth communication between testers and developers and moderates meetings and seminars in which defects are identified and resolved. In addition, he develops, runs and maintains automated test scripts and analyzes and fixes any errors that occur.

Daniel has a particular focus on the application of modern test methods. He uses the V-model in combination with flexible approaches such as SCRUM and SAFe to create an agile yet structured test environment. These methods allow him to continuously improve the quality of software testing while reacting flexibly to changing project requirements.

Thanks to his extensive experience and test automation skills, Daniel helps to accelerate testing processes while ensuring their accuracy and reliability. Through his work, project teams can achieve their quality goals more efficiently and identify and correct errors at an early stage.

Erwin Moretz
Testers and test automation specialists
Erwin Moretz has 7 years of experience in software testing and 3 years in test automation. In his projects, he uses various test techniques to ensure that errors are detected and rectified at an early stage. His main tasks include the creation of test cases and test data, as well as the acceptance of customer requirements from the specialist department.

He is responsible for the execution of tests in various phases, including integration tests, system tests, acceptance tests, regression tests, load and performance tests as well as end-to-end tests (E2E). Erwin documents the test results, creates defect reports and tracks their resolution.

A particular focus is on the automation of tests. Erwin is responsible for the new development and setup of automated tests, especially by using the capture & replay approach to efficiently replace manual test executions. He also has experience in creating automated test cases with JavaScript for microservices based on REST and GraphQL endpoints.

As an agile tester, Erwin has extensive project experience in creating technical specifications, test cases and test data for manual and automated tests. By using agile methods, he ensures flexible and fast adaptations to changing project requirements, which increases the efficiency and quality of the test processes.

His work in test automation contributes significantly to the acceleration of test processes and the reduction of errors, which leads to greater efficiency and product quality.
